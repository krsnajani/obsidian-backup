From - [[@r.everhartPolicyNoteCurrent2026|Jonathan R. Everhart. Intertax. 2026]]

The paper makes the following argument:
1. That both the UN and the OECD lack in providing a forum where all nations, and supra-national groups can have a seat at the table when developing international tax policy
2. While the OECD is known to have a limited membership, their methods of including non-members have also proved less than ideal in providing a broad-base representation to developing countries
3. The UN, while having a mode inclusive mandate fails to involve all third world nations in the workings of the CoE (Comm. of Experts). So for member nations there exists a crisis of oversight
4. There are two possible solutions in the existing literature
	- REC's - These are regional groups forming nations which often come together due to similar economic aspirations, 
	- Political Forum: Since politics has an important role to play in IT, determining various issues concerning place of taxation, characterisation etc. 
- The paper proposes that the UN High Level Forum on IT should be an organisation formed under ECOSOC, modelled after the Forum on Sustainable Development which aims to provide representation to the developing states.
