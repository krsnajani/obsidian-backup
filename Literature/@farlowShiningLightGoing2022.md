<!-- START_ABSTRACT -->
Around the world governments are introducing highly contentious decryption laws to assist with law enforcement and national security goals. This paper analyses Australia’s Telecommunications and Other Legislation (Assistance and Access) Bill 2020 (TOLA) as a vehicle for interrogating the international decryption debate. It analyses the submissions to the 2020 Review of the amendments made by the Telecommunications and Other Legislation Amendment (Assistance and Access) Act 2018 to identify issues of contention and debate among stakeholders. It proposes a Proportionality Framework drawn from international human rights law as a means to balance the competing claims of stakeholders. The framework offers a tool to assess, co-design and communicate decryption law.
