<!-- START_ABSTRACT -->
The Government of India and the CBDT rolled out one of the most ambitious reforms in 2012 – the introduction of the Advance Pricing Agreement (APA) programme. The intention behind taking this step was to dismantle the idea of India as jurisdiction where the tax administration ran an aggressive transfer pricing regime. This reform was also aimed at providing certainty to MNEs in respect of their transfer pricing of international transactions, while providing them an incentive to invest in India. These ideas have slowly manifested themselves into reality. Hundreds of APAs have been signed in the last seven odd years, which in turn has extended comfort and certainty to MNEs.
