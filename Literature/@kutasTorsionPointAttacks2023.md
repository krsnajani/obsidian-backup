<!-- START_ABSTRACT -->
Isogeny-based cryptography is a promising approach for postquantum cryptography. The best-known protocol following that approach is the supersingular isogeny Diﬃe-Hellman protocol (SIDH); this protocol was turned into the CCA-secure key encapsulation mechanism SIKE, which was submitted to and remains in the third round of NIST’s postquantum standardization process as an “alternate” candidate.
