The major political parties support the tenet of the original
GST
agreement that
GST
change requires unanimous state approval. However,
GST
change could differentially affect state economies, and thus influence support from individual states. We investigate the potential for
GST
change to differentially affect state economies. We do this by developing a multi‐regional model of the Australian economy that contains details of the legislated features of the
GST
. In this model, when we change any element of the
GST
, the economic effects are informed by regional differences in economic structure and their interactions with the details of our
GST
theory.
