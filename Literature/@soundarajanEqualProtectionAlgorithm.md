<!-- START_ABSTRACT -->
Within the ﬁeld of ethical machine learning, an area of special concern is the possibility of machine learning algorithms discriminating against groups of people in unethical ways, such as targeting advertisements based on race. In this paper, we propose a framework based on long-standing U.S. legal principles to determine whether the targeting of a group should be viewed with suspicion. Unlike existing work, we are focused on the case when the group is not correlated with known ‘protected features’, or such data is unavailable.
