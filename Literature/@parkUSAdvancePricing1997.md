<!-- START_ABSTRACT -->
Obtaining a preliminary approval from its tax authority as to its mehodologies of transfer pricing would provide taxpayers with a safe harbor. This preliminary measure provides positive assurance against unexpected income allocation adjustments made by the tax authority. Such a procedure is commonly referred to as an “Advance Pricing Agreement(APA).” In order to be an effective income allocation method, an APA must be an either binational or multinational. Only binational or multinational APA could supplement the judicial, administrative, and treaty mechanisms for resolving transfer pricing issues related to income allocation when the traditional mehtods fails or are difficult to apply.
