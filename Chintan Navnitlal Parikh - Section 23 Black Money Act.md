## Chintan Navnitlal Parikh v. PDIT
- The Father of Assessee sets up a [[discretionary trust]], as the [[settlor]] in 1990. It is the case of the Assessee that he was informed about the existence of the trust after the death of his Father, in 2006. 
- When the BMA came into force in 2015 there was a transition scheme in place, wherein the Assessee must pay the tax and the penalty for an undisclosed foreign assets that were so declared by 31st December 2015. Those who do so, will not assessed under the Act. 
- In compliance with this scheme, the Assessee had made a disclosure of 8.55 Crs which was 1/3rd share of what was left from the corpus of the trust. The other two beneficiaries were his spouse and his son. 
- The case was settled for assessment and the disclosure was examined under Section 10(3) of the BMA which is concerned with the assessment of undisclosed foreign income. The sum payable was determined and subsequently **paid by the Assessee**. 
- Later a SCN under Section 23 of the Act was made, under sub-section (2) to explain the claims that were made in a particular document obtained by the PDIT, where, in a minutes of meeting supposedly undertaken by the trustee's of Nutshel Trust, it was stated that the Assessee has taken some amount of money from the [[trust corpus]] during 1990-2006 and that **disclosure of this account is pending + it was found that the Assessee has 3 foreign bank accounts which he did not disclose**
- To this, it was responded that the MoM in question was not in the knowledge of the Assessee and that there was nothing that could suggest a nexus. It had been unilaterally signed by Mr. Brun who appears to be a trustee, and apart from the name of the Assessee there is nothing that ties him to this meeting which was conducted in **2007**. It was further argued that
	- Did not know of such a trust
	- It was not signed by him or any member of the family
	- There is nothing in the MoM that suggests knowledge of this meeting with the Assessee
	- Ratification of transaction took place without the consent of the beneficiaries. 
	- He has also not received the amount in question. This factum stated in the MoM cannot be corroborated by bank statements. 
	- The management of the trust is made by a [[independent trust company]] and the beneficiaries have no decision making power. 
- Further it was contended that the 3 bank accounts in question did not belong the Assessee in the first place, and that they were held by Minerva Trusts which was the [[independent trust company]].
- Though, the PDIT not being satisfied by the disclosure and explanation provided made a revision order u/s 23 sub-section (4) by making an addition of 15,85,82,577 to the original assessment. 
### Submissions
- It was submitted that the order under section 23, could only be made if the twin conditions laid down also for revisions made u/s 263 of the IT Act are fulfilled. These twin conditions are that 
	1. It must be proved that the order was erroneous
	2. It must be proved that it was against the interests of the Department
	Reference in this regard ay be had to [[Parashuram Pottery Works|Parashuram Pottery Works Co. Ltd v. ITO]]
- In the present case it is not erroneous, because the the AO has taken a 'possible view', whether that view is plausible or not, is not within the capacity of the CIT to check and determine. 
- Further once something has been examined, there is no question of calling it back into question - [[CIT v. Nirma Chemicals]]
- There cannot be an initiation of inquiry on the pretext of a inadequate inquiry, which in the present case meant lacking the documents with respect of such a MoM, or disclosures concerning the bank accounts in question. - [[Inadequacy of an inquiry is not grounds for passing order under s. 263 of the Act|Sunbeam]]
