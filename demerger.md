It involves the transfer of an undertaking from a demerged company, to a resulting company. The demerged company in this case is thus the company whose undertaking is transferred, and the resulting company is the one to whom the undertaking is transferred. A resulting company need not be a "new company", therefore an undertaking can also be "demerged" into an existing company. See [[Scheme of the Hinduja Demerger.canvas|the Hinduja Demerger]] and [[Whether GAAR can recharacterize an NCLT approved scheme of demerger|the Bombay High Court's decision on this]]

**Definition in Section 2(19AA)**
## Tax Neutral
A demerger can become 'tax neutral' on fulfilling certain pre-requisites
1. That the property relatable to the undertaking must be transferred to the resulting company
2. That the transfer must be at book value
3. That the consideration for the transfer must be that the resulting company issues shares to the shareholders of the demerged company. 
4. At least 3/4th shareholders in the demerged company become shareholders in the resulting company
5. Transfer of the undertaking is on a going concern basis i.e it is active, fully operational, and self-sustaining business

## Benefits
- Section 72A(4) provides that unabsorbed depreciation, and accumulated losses are allowed to be transferred in the hands of the resultant company to the extant that they could be attributed to the resultant company. 
- If it cannot be attributed to such an undertaking, then it must apportioned on a pro-rata basis looking at the proportion in which the assets are apportioned between the two entities.
