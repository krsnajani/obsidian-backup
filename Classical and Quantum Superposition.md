In classical physics, superposition is when two quantities are added in order to make an entirely distinct third quantity. This is most intuitively understood  in the context of waves or vectors. NC Headphones work on this principle where sound of the same magnitude is used to cancel and silence the outside sound.

In quantum physics, when a coin is being flipped it is in the state of superposition, when it lands it assumes a definite value and becomes observable. In Schrödinger's thought experiment the cat is both alive and dead unless the box is opened. It is said to be in the 'superposition' of the two binaries. Upon measurement, the state becomes definite. 

Although a qubit can have infinite superposition states, it is only possible to extract a single classical value from it. Information can only be obtained through measurement, and doing so limits its states one of the two states. Here the outcomes is the same as classical bits.  The reason why they make computing on the whole faster on quantum computers is because of [[Massive Parallelism]]

## Notation

Any qubit $\psi$ can be represented as the superposition of 1 and 0 and in this particular bra-ket manner.

$$ψ⟩= α|0⟩+ β|1⟩$$
Here the $\alpha$ and $\beta$ represent **amplitudes**, which refer to the *mathematical representation of the all the possible superpositions*. It is also a way to analyse the possibility and probability a state.

$$|0\rangle = |\alpha|^2$$

$$|1\rangle = |\beta|^2$$

And because the probability of observing all the states must be 100%, $|\beta| + |\alpha| = 1$
