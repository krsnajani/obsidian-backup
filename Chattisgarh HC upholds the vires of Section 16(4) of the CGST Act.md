## Jain Brothers

### Submissions
- Disallowing ITC for delay in filing a return and claiming ITC frustrates the object of GST
- If the RP fulfills the objectives of (2) but does not file within (4), the ITC has to be allowed
- It violates Art. 14 beause it has far beyond the scope and object of the Act. 
- One of the key features is to have an uninterrupted chaim of ITC. This object is defeated if (4) is applied strictly, thus it violates Art. 14 for being repugnant to the object sought by the statute
- Section 16(4) disallows the ITC merely based on time lines for those taxes which are already paid for purchase of goods and/or services as the case may be.
- It also violates 19(1)(g) because it it is nowhere covered under (6)
- Section 39 of the Act even permits the return to be filed even after the due date following the end of the financial year and thus the *intention of the law is not to bar the ITC by time lines by any means and thus the present provision is bad in law*
