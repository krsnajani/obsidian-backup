The follow issue has arisen in respect of a [[demerger]] of NXTDIGITAL into Hinduja Global Solutions Ltd.  ([[Scheme of the Hinduja Demerger.canvas |see Scheme]])

> Note : In so far as T-3 is concerned, there is a distinction between a demerger and a slump sale. T-2 is a [[slump sale]] where the healthcare division has been acquired for cash, whereas T-3 is a demerger whereby there is no "consideration" in the transaction per se, but HGS has issued shares to the shareholders in NXTDIGITAL

In effect the question is, whether the 'economic substance' doctrine can override a statutory provision. The position in [[@justicer.mahadevanAuthorityAdvanceRulings2026]] and [[@justicemoritzLibertyGlobalInc2026]] are both an interesting position. Justice Moritz in Liberty Global is of the opinion that 'economic substance' is not merely an interpretative tool, but can also override the tax provisions. 

The argument overwhelmingly reiterated in this case is that - "Since there is a provision for demerger sanctioned under the Act, it cannot constitute an 'impermissible avoidance agreement'". Both Tiger and Liberty Global take a position that favours the Revenue on this issue.
