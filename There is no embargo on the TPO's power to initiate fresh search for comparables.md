The short issue is, whether the TPO can undertake a fresh search of new comparables, having without having rejected the TPSR of the assessee. Consequently, are there any limitations on the TPO which limits it from undertaking such search?

There three cases worth considering

- **Mentor Graphics** - Where it has been held that it was not possible for the TPO to have undertaken a new search without deciding that the comparables drawn by the taxpayer were insufficient or had any other deficiency. After *Mentor Graphics* this issue has been constantly pled before the ITAT, but has not been given any serious consideration. 

	- **Mentor Graphics Appeal** - In appeal before the HC, the Court diluted the findings of the ITAT holding that position of the Tribunal will have to tempered in light of the statutory provisions which are set out in 92C(3). These stipulate four situations where the TPO may proceed to determine ALP. If any of the four conditions has been satisfied it would be open to the AO/TPO to determine the ALP. The Court positioned this observation as a "clarification" to the order of the Tribunal.[@MentorGraphicsLtd2007]
	
- **Phillips Software** - Here three observations are worth considering. 

	1. That a TPO cannot undertake a fresh assessment without having proved that any of the grounds under 92C(3) existed. Even if an infirmity is identified, it only has a remedial function by which it may modify the analysis of the taxpayer only to the extent that the computation of the ALP deviates from sub-sections (1) and (2) of section 92C. In certain cases, for instance non-compliance with 92C(3)(a), the AO/TPO must remedy the assessment commensurate to the infirmity found, on the basis of the data given by the assessee, rather than conducting a fresh search. 
	
	2. That once the assessee has undertaken a proper comparability analysis, it would be open for the AO to make a fresh analysis only if the case has fallen under 92C(3)(c).  (also see Circular No. 14/2001 issued by the CBDT). That factum in the present case has not been satisfied. 
	
	3. Finally, that the TPO must strictly comply with the requirements of Rule 10C, whereby it is mandatory for the taxpayer to ensure that the data which exists is of the **relevant financial year**, and that it **exists at the given time.** If these conditions are not satisfied, the order must be scrapped at the threshold. 

- **Willis Processing Services** - Here it was argued that the TPO should not have undertaken a fresh search for comparables, once it had accepted the 8 comparables selected by the assessee. The Bombay ITAT held that Section 92CA empowers the TPO  to gather relevant information, documents, for determining the ALP in relation to an international transaction. There is no embargo on the powers of the TPO in carrying out a fresh search under the Act.


A point to be noted here, is that Phillips Software and Mentor Graphics (HC) do not sit in conflict with each other. While any of the grounds under (3) allow the AO to determine the ALP, Phillips Software posits that this power must also be used within its limits, and gives a nuanced view of the position taken in Mentor Graphics (HC).
