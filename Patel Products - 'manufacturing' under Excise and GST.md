The Gujarat High Court on the 5th of January gave a significant judgement which has introduced serious uncertainty for the Tobacco industry. While crushed and sieved tobacco was characterised as 'unmanufactured' under the Central Excise Regime, Justice Karia has held that because the CGST Act specifically defines 'manufacturing' in unambiguous terms, the process of creating the 'subject goods' fall under 'manufactured' tobacco in the GST Regime. The decision comes at a time when the entire tax structure for the tobacco regime is being subject to substantial changes. This is a short birds-eye perspective of the decision and the positions that resonated with the Court. 

# Patel Products & Anr v. Union of India [**2025-VIL-1358-GUJ**]

## Facts
- The Petitioner was in the business of supplying raw, unmanufactured and unprocessed tobacco and obtained registration under the provisions of Central Excise Act and registration on the certificate of the subject product as under Tarrif Heading 2401 through the jurisdictional officer in Gujarat. 
- This was in pursuance of a circular dt. 23-06-1987 issued by the CBEC clarifying that unmanufactured tobacco which was sieved and packed in retail packets would be classifiable under 2401 as 'unmanufactured tobacco'.
- In the aftermath of GST, the petitioner migrated registration as supplier of 'unmanufactured tobacco' under heading 2401 from 01/07/2017. 
- During the course of the audit conducted (Audit u/s 65 of the CGST Act) the Department issued Form EA-2000 (on the completion of the audit), in which no allegation regarding classification was raised. 
- But the DG Intelligence (GST) Ahmedabad, choose to conduct a search at the premises of the Petitioner and subsequently issued a SCN for the demand and recovery of 
	1. Compensation Cess of Rs. 412,48,78,774/- 
	2. GST of Rs. 17,86,80,879/-
- The Petitioners challenged the SCN by preferring a writ on the ground of being arbitrary and in excess of jurisdiction
- The Court vide interim order (dt. 27/11/2024) permitted the Petitioner to raise the contentions before the 'adjudicating authority' and directed the Department to pass an order after providing the Petitioner an opportunity to be heard. 
- The Commissioner, Central Tax thereafter passed an order finally classifying the Petitioner's product under heading 2403 instead of 2401.

### Rival Contentions
- The GST and Compensation Cess is levied under GST by their respective notifications wherein it adopts the First Schedule of the Customs Tariff Act. The Fourth Schedule of the *Central Excise Act* and the First Schedule of the Customs Tariff Act are harmonised and aligned. Thus, **all of the directions, clarifications issued under the excise regime will be relevant to the scope of the tariff headings under the GST regime.**
- The CBEC in circular F. No. 81/5/87-CX.3 clarified that tobacco products obtained merely by *beating, sieving and packing* in the retail packets with or without brand names would be classified under 2401 of First Schedule to the CE Act as 'unmanufactured tobacco'. The same extends to Central Excise Tariff by virtue of the F. No. 81/01/2015-CX.3. Thus, it was submitted that subject goods of the petitioner are crushed and sieved by tobacco leaves and thus reading the given notifications within the context of the relevant GST notifications purports that the *central excise classification must also extend to the GST regime*.
- Merely repacking raw tobacco from bulk packages into retail packs and labelling them with a brand name would not convert unmanufactured tobacco into manufactured tobacco. It was argued that this has been a consistent holding of the CESTAT Ahmedabad and Delhi also (Jaikishan Tobacco, and Sureh Tobacco) where it was held that the appellants raw material as well as the finished goods are cut leaves from the natural leaves of tobacco therefore being 'unmanufactured tobacco'. In order to come under 2403, it will have to undergo the process of manufacturing.
- It was also argued that the Petitioner did not undertake any *manufacturing* as it was only involved in repacking the unmanufactured tobacco into smaller retail pouches. The process of converting bulk to retail cannot by itself constitute as 'manufacture' for the purpose of GST.
- The consumption method alone would not determine whether the product is classified as 'chewing tobacco' under 'manufactured' heading. The sole basis of the claim is that the product in question is used for 'chewing purpose' which is erroneous. (Commissioner of Central Excise v. M/s Madhan Agro Industries (India))
