## Change to Art. 25
- A new paragraph 6 is added to Article 25, which discusses the implication of Paragraph 3 of Art. XXII (22) of the General Agreement on Trade in Services (GATS) which is part of Annex 1B to the Marrakesh Agreement. 
- Art. 22 of the GATS is concerned with 'Consultation' of which Paragraph 3 is specifically concerned with an exception. The Article prescribes that each member shall afford an opportunity to consult with the other Member States with respect to a matter effecting the operation of the Agreement. Even the Dispute Settlement Body may enter into consultation with the Member in order to resolve any issues that may be unresolved by bilateral or multilateral consultation. 
	- Paragraph 3 exempts disputes where a dispute arises with respect to a violation of Art. 17 on account of a rule covered by a DTAA. Art. 17 establishes the NT (National Treatment) principle requiring both domestic and foreign goods and service providers to be treated equally. 
	- If any foreign establishment, appears to be prejudiced by virtue of a DTAA, the GATS Dispute Settlement Understanding as under Art. XXIII (23) shall not be applicable. But if both members do not agree as to whether the dispute is covered under the DTAA, it is open for such Member to bring it before the Council for Trade in Services which may refer it to arbitration.
- The OECD clarifies that a measure is said to fall within the Model Convention, only if it is a measure to which provisions of Art. 24 apply. 
	- Art. 24 is concerned with Non-Discrimination, and it is stated that
		1. Nationals of a Contracting State shall not be subjected in the other Contracting State to any taxation  ==which is other or more burdensome than the taxation==  to ==which nationals of that other State== in the same circumstances, are or ==may be subjected==.  
		2. Stateless persons who are not residents of a Contracting State shall not be subject to any taxation that is more burdensome than the taxation to which the nationals of that State are subjected.
		3. Taxation on a permanent establishment shall not be less favourably levied, as against other establishments in that State which are carrying out the same activities. That means no allowances, or tax benefits can be provided to an establishment which is not also extended to a PE. 
		4. Deductions on the taxable profits shall be calculated in the same manner for the residents of Contracting States, as if they were calculated for the residents of the other Contracting State. 
		5. Enterprises in a Contracting State which are partly held by one or more residents of another Contracting State shall not be prejudicially taxed as against other similarly placed enterprises in such State.
