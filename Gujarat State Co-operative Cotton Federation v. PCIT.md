> Whether the order of the AO permitting deduction under 80P can be subject to revision under 263

The issue before the ITAT was whether the interest that was obtained by the GUJCOT could be eligible for an exemption under Section 80P of the Act. The facts of the case were:

- That GUJCOT is a nodal agency that has been notified by the National Agricultural Cooperative Marketing Federation of India (NAFED) to procure seeds and pulses and to disburse the MSP pricing to such farmers in Gujarat.
- In this process, money was obtained by it from NAFED which was to be disbursed to the farmers, and apposite to maintaining the security of these funds, they were placed into a Fixed Deposit
- From such a deposit certain interest was earned by the Assessee and this interest was given an exemption under Section 80P of the Act. The Assessee had already been claiming an interest for the preceding two years, though this was not permitted in the year under consideration. 
- The Revisional Commissioner took it up as a case under 263 claiming that because the income in question is from interest, it can only be claimed under 80P(2)(d) and revised the Order by disallowing the claim.

## Grounds for Appeal
- The main claim was that the Commissioner could not have invalidated a view which was **possible** merely on the grounds that it is not **plausible**. 
- Having fulfilled the grounds under 2(a)(iii), it is clear that the position taken by the AO was indeed a possible position, it need not be thus subject to revision unless it is shown to have been completely erroneous. 
- There are twin conditions that need to be fulfilled in this regard, the first is that it must be an errneous order, and the other is that it must be against the interest of the revenue. (see [[Parashuram Pottery Works]]) similar issues were also raised in [[CIT v. Nirma Chemicals]] and [[Malabar Industrial Co. Ltd]]

## Decision
- It was accepted that the fund had been used as working capital for the operation of purchase, and the remainder had to be paid to the State. In that AY, the assessee had created an FD in the short term on such amount which was to be paid back, and earned interest on the same. 
- When the said fund was idle, it was neither sent back to the Government, nor kept with any other such organisation within GUJCOT, but rather kept with a commercial bank on which interest income was generated. Various aspects regarding the transaction having not been verified by the AO, the Tribunal observed that the Ast. Order was of prejudice to the revenue and thus the appeal of the assessee is dismissed

**In favour of revenue**
